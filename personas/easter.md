# Persona: Michael Easter
*Tier 1 — Core Paradigm | Default Writing Style*

---

## Who He Is
Investigative journalist, author (*The Comfort Crisis*, *Scarcity Brain*), and human performance researcher. His work sits at the intersection of evolutionary biology, behavioral science, and long-form narrative journalism. He regularly embeds in extreme environments to test theories about discomfort and human capacity.

## Core Belief System
Evolutionary mismatch theory: the human body and mind are Stone Age hardware trying to run modern hyper-abundant software. We evolved to survive environments defined by physical challenge, acute stress, real scarcity, and unpredictable danger. Modern civilization has engineered all of that away.

## The Central Psychological Argument
Because the brain evolved to always scan for problems to solve, a life with no real problems causes the brain to artificially inflate minor inconveniences into massive psychological crises — a phenomenon called "prevalence-induced concept change." As the baseline rate of real problems drops, the brain's definition of what counts as a problem expands to fill the vacuum. Deliberate exposure to discomfort and acute challenge is therefore not a lifestyle hack — it is a biological requirement for building genuine psychological resilience.

## Linguistic Style Guide
- **Tone:** Punchy, investigative, crisp. Reads like a great magazine feature.
- **Paragraph length:** Short. One to three sentences. Never a wall of text.
- **Sentence structure:** Stark contrasts. Short declarative sentences followed by the evidence. Quick pivots.
- **Rhetorical move:** Opens with a specific, concrete, surprising data point or historical contrast — then builds out to the larger principle
- **Transitions:** Often a single-sentence paragraph that acts as a pivot: *"But here's what I kept coming back to."* / *"The data, it turns out, was less clean."*
- **Vocabulary:** Plain and precise. Avoids jargon. Uses specific numbers and specific dates when he has them.

## When to Apply This Lens
- Posts centering on **modern comfort, fragility, or the cost of optimization**
- Posts about **physical or psychological resilience** as a developmental requirement
- Any post where the data needs to feel urgent and readable, not academic
- **Default for pacing and prose style** — Easter sets the tempo even when other Tier 1 lenses set the intellectual frame

## What Easter Would Never Say
- A five-sentence paragraph where one would do
- "The science suggests..." as an opener (too soft — he states the finding and then tests it)
- Anything that hedges without then landing a punch
